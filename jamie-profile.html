01 Mission

My Product Mission

I want to build products that people actually need, not just ones that ship. At Error404, my job is to turn ambiguity into direction: understanding the problem deeply enough to say no to good ideas in favor of the right one, and holding the line between what users are asking for and what will actually solve their problem. I care about closing the gap between strategy and execution - a roadmap only matters if engineering, design, and the business are pulling in the same direction. Good product leadership isn't about having the best ideas in the room; it's about making sure the right ideas win.

04 Growth

Skills Acquired

The three most valuable skills I've built leading product at Error404.

  • 01 Turning ambiguity into a clear strategy — taking a vague problem or market signal and shaping it into a prioritized roadmap the whole company can rally behind.
  • 02 Leading without owning the work directly — aligning engineering, design, and the business around a shared vision, and earning buy-in through clarity rather than authority.
  • 03 Deciding fast with incomplete information — using data and direct user feedback to make confident calls under uncertainty, then adjusting quickly when the evidence changes.
05 Energy

Energy & Engagement

What energizes me most is the 0-to-1 stretch — the point where a problem is still fuzzy and the team is deciding what to actually build. Sitting with users to understand what they really need, pressure-testing a roadmap against real constraints, and watching a product go from a whiteboard sketch to something people rely on daily: that's the work I come back for. I'm especially engaged when a decision is genuinely hard — where the data is incomplete and the team is looking to product to set direction anyway.

06 Reflection

Looking Back

If I could give my early-career self one piece of advice, it would be: talk to users before you fall in love with your own idea. It's tempting to lead with a solution you're excited about, but the roadmaps I'm proudest of started with a problem I understood better than anyone else in the room. The other lesson: influence matters more than title. Getting a team aligned around a hard call has almost never come down to authority — it's come down to being clear, being honest about trade-offs, and being willing to change my mind when the evidence says I should.

07 Gratitude

Circle of Influence

I'm grateful to the mentors who challenged my thinking early on, the founders and leaders at Error404 who trusted me with hard calls, and every engineer, designer, and teammate who pushed back on an idea until it was actually good. Their honesty, patience, and collaboration have shaped both how I lead and the kind of product leader I'm still becoming.
